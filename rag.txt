Principle of Operation

When 3-Φ supply is given to the 3-Φ stator winding, a constant amplitude rotating magnetic field is produced.  
The speed of rotation of the magnetic field depends on the number of stator poles and the supply frequency.

This speed is called synchronous speed.
Ns = 120f / P


The MMF produced by both stator and rotor travel in the same direction at synchronous speed.

Combination of these two MMFs gives a resultant air-gap MMF or flux density wave rotating at synchronous speed.
Since the relative speed between rotor MMF and the resultant flux density wave is zero, a steady torque is developed by their interaction.

Stator and Rotor Structure
Stator:
- 3-Φ distributed winding

Rotor:
- Rotor bars

Between them is the air gap through which flux passes.

Air-gap flux (Φ)
Assume Xₗ = 0
Flux Φ produces MMF (F₂)  
MMF produces current (I₂)  
Current produces EMF (E₂)


When 3-Φ supply is given to a 3-Φ winding, two poles N₁ and S₁ are produced.  
A resultant flux density wave is created in the stator and rotates in anti-clockwise direction at synchronous speed.

Flux passes through the air gap and cuts the stationary rotor bars at synchronous speed.
Rotor bars at pole centers have maximum EMF, and bars away from the pole center have reduced EMF magnitude.

Magnitude of EMF is proportional to the relative velocity between the flux and rotor conductors.
|EMF| ∝ Vrelative

According to Fleming's Right Hand Rule the EMF direction is determined.
Since rotor bars are short-circuited, rotor current flows according to Lenz's law.

To reduce the relative speed between the rotating flux of the stator and the stationary rotor conductors, the rotor starts rotating in the same direction as the flux to catch up the speed, but it cannot catch it and rotates at a speed:
Nr < Ns

If the rotor circuit is assumed to be purely resistive, the rotor current is in phase with the EMF generated E2.
The current in rotor conductors produces MMF F2 which lags the air-gap flux by 90°.

By interaction of air-gap flux and rotor MMF F2, both are in the same direction of the rotating magnetic field.
As a result, electromagnetic torque will be developed.

Torque relations:
T ∝ Φ F2 sin 90°
T ∝ Φ Ia   (DC machine)
T ∝ Φ I cosθ   (AC machine)

In an actual machine, the rotor conductors are placed in rotor slots which are made up of magnetic material.
Rotor conductors offer leakage reactance.

As a result the rotor becomes inductive.
Rotor current in each bar lags behind the generated EMF by rotor power factor angle θ₂.
θ₂ = tan⁻¹ (X₂ / R₂)

Rotor leakage reactance due to current in rotor bars becomes maximum only when poles N1 and S1 have travelled through an angle θ₂ from the position of maximum EMF.

Rotor current sets up rotor MMF F2 which is displaced from air-gap flux Φ by an angle of:
90° + θ₂

Rotor power factor:
cos θ₂

Torque equation considering rotor power factor:
T ∝ Φ F2 sin (90 + θ₂)
T ∝ Φ F2 cos θ₂

Design Equation

Q = C₀ D² L ns
Where:
C₀ → Output coefficient  
D → Diameter  
L → Axial length  
ns → Speed (rps)

C₀ = (Specific electric loading) × (Specific magnetic loading)
D²L → Frame size of machine


In case of squirrel cage induction motor, since the rotor is short circuited, the rotor can adjust itself to any number of stator poles.

But in case of wound rotor induction motor, both rotor and stator windings must be wound for the same number of poles.

Rotor Frequency (f₂)

At starting (standstill)
Nr = 0

The frequency of the induced EMF in the rotor is the same as that of supply frequency fs.
When the rotor starts rotating at a speed Nr (rpm) in the direction of the rotating flux wave, the relative speed between the rotating flux and rotor conductor becomes:

ns − nr

Supply frequency
f₁ = (P Ns) / 120

Rotor frequency
f₂ = (P / 120) (ns − nr)
f₂ = (P / 2) (ns − nr)

Slip speed
ns − nr

Slip
s = (ns − nr) / ns

Percentage slip
% slip = ((ns − nr) / ns) × 100

ns − nr = s * ns

Rotor frequency
f₂ = (P / 2) (s ns)
f₂ = s f₁

At starting:
Slip s = 1
Therefore
f₂ = f₁

When the rotor is revolving at speed Nr:
fr = s f₁

The rotor produces a magnetic field revolving at a speed s ns with respect to the rotor structure.
But the rotor itself is already revolving at mechanical speed Nr with respect to the stator.

Therefore:

Speed of rotor field with respect to stator
= Mechanical rotor speed (Nr)  
+ Rotor field speed with respect to rotor (s ns)

Rotor Impedance

Rotor impedance at standstill (per phase):
Z2 = r2 + jx2
|Z2| = √(r2^2 + x2^2)

Rotor impedance at any slip (s):
|Z2| = √(r2^2 + (s x2)^2)

--------------------------------------------------

Rotor Current

Rotor current at standstill:
I2 = E2 / √(r2^2 + x2^2)

Rotor current at any slip (s):
I2 = sE2 / √(r2^2 + (s x2)^2)

--------------------------------------------------

Rotor Circuit Phase Angle

Rotor current lags rotor voltage (E2) by angle θ2:

θ2 = tan^-1 (s x2 / r2)

--------------------------------------------------

Speed of Rotor Field

Speed of rotor field w.r.t stator:

Nr = Ns

Derivation:
Nr = Ns(1 - s) + sNs
Nr = Ns

--------------------------------------------------

Key Points

- Rotor physical speed is less than stator field speed
- Stator field w.r.t rotor moves at synchronous speed
- Rotor and stator fields are stationary w.r.t each other
- Relative speed between fields = 0
- Steady torque is produced

--------------------------------------------------

Rotor Circuit Relations

Rotor induced emf at slip (s):
E2(s) = sE2

Rotor frequency:
f2 = s f1

Rotor reactance:
x2(s) = 2π f2 L2
      = 2π (s f1) L2
      = s x2

--------------------------------------------------

Diagram Summary

Two rotor equivalent circuits:

1. Standstill:
   - Resistance = r2
   - Reactance = x2
   - EMF = E2

2. At slip (s):
   - Resistance = r2
   - Reactance = s x2
   - EMF = sE2

→ Shows how rotor parameters vary with slip

Power Flow in Rotor

Pin to rotor = Stator output
             = Air-gap power
             = Pg
             = E2 I2 cosθ2

--------------------------------------------------

Power Factor

cosθ2 = (r2/s) / √((r2/s)^2 + x2^2)

--------------------------------------------------

Air-Gap Power (Pg)

Pg = E2 × I2 × cosθ2

Substituting:

Pg = E2 × [E2 / √((r2/s)^2 + x2^2)] × [(r2/s) / √((r2/s)^2 + x2^2)]

Pg = (E2^2 × (r2/s)) / [(r2/s)^2 + x2^2]

Since:
I2 = E2 / √((r2/s)^2 + x2^2)

⇒ Pg = I2^2 × (r2/s)

★ Pg = (I2^2 r2) / s

--------------------------------------------------

Rotor Input Power

Power input to rotor:

Pg = Rotor Cu loss / s

Pg = I2^2 r2 / s

--------------------------------------------------

Power Distribution

Pg = Rotor Cu loss + Mechanical power developed

Pg = I2^2 r2 + I2^2 r2 ((1 - s)/s)

--------------------------------------------------

Mechanical Power Developed

Pm = Pg (1 - s)

Pm = (I2^2 r2 / s) (1 - s)

Pm = I2^2 r2 (1/s - 1)

--------------------------------------------------

Power Relation

Pg = sPg + Pg(1 - s)
   = sPg + Pm

--------------------------------------------------

Rotor Copper Loss

Rotor Cu loss = s × Rotor input power
              = sPg

--------------------------------------------------

Torque Developed

Internal torque developed per phase:

T = Pm / ωr
  = Pg(1 - s) / ωr

Where:

ωs = (2π Ns) / 60
ωr = (2π Nr) / 60

--------------------------------------------------

Torque Relation

Pm / ωr = Pg / ωs

⇒ T = Pg / ωs

⇒ T = (I2^2 r2) / (s ωs)

--------------------------------------------------

Key Notes

- Stator output = Power transferred to rotor
- Air-gap power splits into:
  • Rotor copper loss
  • Mechanical power developed
- Slip determines power division
- Higher slip → more copper loss
- Lower slip → more mechanical output

Numerical Problem

Given:

Power input (Pin) = 40 kW  
Voltage = 400 V  
Frequency = 60 Hz  
Poles (P) = 6  
Speed (Nr) = 1140 rpm  
Power factor = 0.8 lag  

Stator losses = 1 kW  
Friction & windage losses = 2 kW  

Find:
- Slip
- Rotor Cu loss
- Mechanical power developed
- Efficiency
- Current

--------------------------------------------------

Step 1: Air-gap Power

Pg = Pin - Stator losses  
Pg = 40 - 1 = 39 kW

--------------------------------------------------

Step 2: Synchronous Speed

Ns = (120 × f) / P  
Ns = (120 × 60) / 6 = 1200 rpm

Rotor speed:
Nr = 1140 rpm

--------------------------------------------------

Step 3: Slip

s = (Ns - Nr) / Ns  
s = (1200 - 1140) / 1200  
s = 60 / 1200 = 0.05

--------------------------------------------------

Step 4: Slip

s = (Ns - Nr) / Ns  
s = (1200 - 1140) / 1200

Pout = Pm - F&W losses

= (35.05 / 40) × 100

Pin = √3 VL IL cosφ  

IL = Pin / (√3 VL cosφ)  

IL = (40 × 10^3) / (√3 × 400 × 0.8)  

IL ≈ 72.17 A

Find:

ii) Rotor Cu loss at full load  
iii) Full load speed  
iv) Internal torque developed  
v) Shaft torque  
vi) Efficiency  

--------------------------------------------------

Step 1: Synchronous Speed

Ns = (120 f) / P  
Ns = (120 × 50) / 4  
Ns = 1500 rpm

Continuation of Problem

--------------------------------------------------

Step 2: Convert Line Current to Phase Current (Delta)

At no-load:
INL = 8 / √3 = 4.62 A  

At full load:
IFL = 18 / √3 = 10.39 A  

--------------------------------------------------

Step 3: Stator Copper Loss (Full Load)

Ps = 3 I^2 R  
   = 3 × (10.39)^2 × 1.2  
   = 389 W

--------------------------------------------------

Step 4: Stator Core Loss

Core loss = Pin(no-load) - F&W - Stator Cu loss (no-load)

Stator Cu loss (no-load):
= 3 × (4.62)^2 × 1.2  

Core loss:
= 600 - 420 - [3 × (4.62)^2 × 1.2]  
= 600 - 420 - 77  
= 103 W

--------------------------------------------------

Step 5: Air-Gap Power

Pag = Pin(full load) - Stator Cu loss - Core loss  

Pag = 11200 - 389 - 103  
Pag = 10708 W  
= 10.708 kW

--------------------------------------------------

Step 6: Mechanical Power Developed

Pm = Pout + F&W losses  
Pm = 10 kW + 0.42 kW  
Pm = 10.42 kW

--------------------------------------------------

Step 7: Slip

s = (Pag - Pm) / Pag  

s = (10708 - 10420) / 10708  
s ≈ 0.0269 ≈ 2.69 %

--------------------------------------------------

Step 8: Full Load Speed

Ns = 1500 rpm  

NFL = Ns (1 - s)  
NFL = 1500 (1 - 0.0269)  
NFL ≈ 1460 rpm

--------------------------------------------------

Step 9: Internal Torque

Tint = Pag / ωs  

Tint = (10708 × 60) / (2π × 1500)  
Tint ≈ 68.2 N·m

--------------------------------------------------

Step 10: Shaft Torque

Tshaft = Pout / ωr  

Tshaft = (10000 × 60) / (2π × 1460)  
Tshaft ≈ 65.2 N·m

--------------------------------------------------

Step 11: Efficiency

η = Output / Input  

η = (10 × 10^3) / (11.2 × 10^3)  
η ≈ 89.3 %

--------------------------------------------------

Final Answers

- Stator core loss = 103 W  
- Rotor Cu loss = Pag - Pm = 288 W  
- Full load speed = 1460 rpm  
- Internal torque = 68.2 N·m  
- Shaft torque = 65.2 N·m  
- Efficiency = 89.3 %
